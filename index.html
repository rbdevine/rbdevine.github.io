C02 On the Rise

C02 output into the atmosphere due to human activity has been on the rise in the past 4 decades.

Nature's Role in Balancing C02

Nature currently outputs approximately 771 GT (gigatons) of CO2, but is capeable of aborbing 788 GT, leaving a net -17 GT that it can absorb of the C02 produced by human activity. When natural resources such as the 2.7 sq. miles of rainforest the Amazon basin in south America are reduced due to deforestation, the amount of C02 that can be absorbed is significantly reduced.

Earth's Surface Temperature Rising with CO2

The surface temperature of the earth on both land and water, relatively stable for thousands of years, began a sharp incline as more CO2 is emitted into the atmosphere.

Glacier Surface Area Reduction

The earth glaciers located at the earth's two poles have been dramatically losing surface area in the past few decades. The trend is expected to accelerate in the coming decades. The measurment shown is the cumulative change in mass balance of a set of “reference” glaciers worldwide beginning in 1945. The values represents the average of all the glaciers that were measured. Negative values indicate a net loss of ice and snow compared with the base year of 1945. For consistency, measurements are in meters of water equivalent, which represent changes in the average thickness of a glacier.

Sea Level Rise

As the glaciers melt, their massive volumes are transfromed from ice to water, causing a rise in sea level. Scientist warn that each inch of sea level rise will result in catastrophic consequences for coastal cities. Island nations, such as Kerabas and the Maldives are particularly at risk.
