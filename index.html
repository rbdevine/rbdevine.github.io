C02 On the Rise

C02 output into the atmosphere due to human activity has been on the rise in the past 5 decades.

Nature's Role in Balancing C02

Nature currently outputs approximately 771 GT (gigatons) of CO2, but is capeable of aborbing 788 GT, leaving a net, -17 GT that it can absorb of the CO2 produced by human activity (a number the human-caused CO2 output exceeded decades ago). When natural resources such as the 2.7 sq. miles of rainforest the Amazon basin in south America are reduced due to deforestation, the amount of CO2 that can be absorbed is significantly reduced, leaving even more excess CO2 in the atmosphere.

Earth's Surface Temperature Rising with CO2

As more CO2 is emitted into the atmosphere, the density of CO2 increases, which in turn causes a number of geophysical phenomenon, one of which is the heating up of the earth's surface. Although the earth's surface temperature has gone through many cycles over the broad scale of geological time, it has been relatively stable for the past fews thousand years. That has changed in the last half century as more and more CO2 due to human activity has been released.

Glacier Surface Area Reduction

As the earth has been heating up over the past few decades, the glaciers located at the its two poles have been dramatically losing surface area. This trend is expected to accelerate in the coming decades. Sea Level Rise

As the glaciers melt, their massive volumes are transfromed from ice to water, causing a rise in sea level. Scientist warn that every inch of sea level rise will result in catastrophic consequences for coastal cities. Island nations, such as Kerabas and the Maldives are particularly at risk.
